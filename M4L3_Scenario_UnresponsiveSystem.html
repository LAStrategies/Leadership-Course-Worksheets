The Unresponsive System

You've identified a real problem affecting students of color on your campus — a process that creates barriers others don't face, a resource that's inaccessible, or a practice that communicates who belongs and who doesn't. You've done your homework: you've documented the pattern, gathered some evidence, and talked to other students who've experienced the same thing.

You raised it once, informally, with someone who seemed sympathetic. You got a polite brush-off — "we hear you," "we're working on it," "these things take time." Nothing changed. You want to push further, but you're not sure how — or when it makes sense to escalate from informal advocacy to something more formal.

Speak Now: You have a relationship with someone in the room, data ready, and the decision hasn't been finalized. Act with your framing — name the evidence, invite a conversation.
Wait & Build: You don't yet have a champion, the timing is politically bad, or you need a stronger coalition. Use this window to build before moving formally.
Escalate: Informal channels have been tried and dismissed. The harm is ongoing. You have documented the pattern. Time to move to formal channels — policy, governance, or public accountability.

→ How do you frame this as an institutional opportunity — not a personal grievance?
You've been brushed off once — which means your current framing may not be giving decision-makers a path to say yes. Try rewriting your key message using the framing principle: connect the change to the institution's stated goals. What does the institution already say it cares about?
